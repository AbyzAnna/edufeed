## Section 1: The Enlightenment (Questions 1-12)

### Questions

**1. Which philosopher argued that humans are born as a "blank slate" (tabula rasa)?**
- A) Jean-Jacques Rousseau
- B) John Locke
- C) Thomas Hobbes
- D) Voltaire

**2. What concept did Montesquieu advocate for in "The Spirit of the Laws"?**
- A) Divine right of kings
- B) Separation of powers
- C) Social contract
- D) General will

**3. Which Enlightenment thinker believed that humans in their natural state were brutish and needed a strong government?**
- A) John Locke
- B) Jean-Jacques Rousseau
- C) Thomas Hobbes
- D) Baron de Montesquieu

**4. Voltaire is best known for advocating:**
- A) Absolute monarchy
- B) Religious tolerance and freedom of speech
- C) Return to nature
- D) Communist revolution

**5. The Encyclopedia, a major Enlightenment work, was edited by:**
- A) Voltaire and Montesquieu
- B) Diderot and d'Alembert
- C) Locke and Hobbes
- D) Rousseau and Smith

**6. Which philosopher wrote "The Social Contract" and argued that legitimate political authority comes from a social contract among the people?**
- A) John Locke
- B) Jean-Jacques Rousseau
- C) Thomas Hobbes
- D) Immanuel Kant

**7. Adam Smith's "The Wealth of Nations" (1776) advocated for:**
- A) Mercantilism
- B) Socialism
- C) Free market capitalism (laissez-faire)
- D) Feudalism

**8. Which concept describes the Enlightenment belief in using reason to discover natural laws governing society?**
- A) Divine revelation
- B) Scientific rationalism
- C) Romanticism
- D) Mysticism

**9. Mary Wollstonecraft's "A Vindication of the Rights of Woman" argued for:**
- A) Women's suffrage only
- B) Equal education for women
- C) Women's exclusion from politics
- D) Return to traditional gender roles

**10. The term "philosophe" refers to:**
- A) Ancient Greek philosophers
- B) French Enlightenment intellectuals
- C) German idealists
- D) British empiricists

**11. Which Enlightenment idea most directly influenced the American Declaration of Independence?**
- A) Hobbes' absolutism
- B) Locke's natural rights (life, liberty, property)
- C) Rousseau's general will
- D) Montesquieu's climate theory

**12. Cesare Beccaria's "On Crimes and Punishments" advocated for:**
- A) Harsher criminal penalties
- B) Reform of criminal justice and opposition to torture
- C) Public executions
- D) Trial by ordeal

### Section 1 Answers
1. B) John Locke
2. B) Separation of powers
3. C) Thomas Hobbes
4. B) Religious tolerance and freedom of speech
5. B) Diderot and d'Alembert
6. B) Jean-Jacques Rousseau
7. C) Free market capitalism (laissez-faire)
8. B) Scientific rationalism
9. B) Equal education for women
10. B) French Enlightenment intellectuals
11. B) Locke's natural rights (life, liberty, property)
12. B) Reform of criminal justice and opposition to torture

20. A) Leading independence movements in South America
21. B) Nationalism, liberalism, and demands for political reform

## Quick Reference: Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Enlightenment | 18th-century intellectual movement emphasizing reason, science, and individual rights |
| Social Contract | Theory that government derives authority from agreement among the governed |
| Laissez-faire | Economic policy of minimal government intervention |
| Nationalism | Ideology based on loyalty to a nation defined by shared culture/language |
| Proletariat | Working class in Marxist theory |
| Bourgeoisie | Capitalist/middle class who own means of production |
| Means of Production | Factories, machinery, land used to produce goods |
| Industrial Revolution | Transition from agrarian to industrial economy (c. 1760-1840) |
| Realpolitik | Politics based on practical objectives rather than ideals |
